What have I worked on?

AskHR

AskHR is a chrome extension which can seach various website of Persistent Systems and saves a ton of employees time

I got the idea for this extension as soon as I joined Persistent Systems. Persistent has different websites for different purposes. For example a different one for each of these : Employee management, HR Management, Persistent blogs, Persistent university etc. I also observed that employees have to spent 5 to 10 mins just to look simple things up becuase these website are pain to navigate even though employees know what they are looking for. As an example looking for their resume on Persistent portal. So I decided to create an easily accessible chrome extension which can make their life easier.

Technologies: Javascript, Chrome extensions, Jquery, mySQL, PHP

CodeConvict

CodeCovict (in progress) is a blogging platfom for developers.

Even though it might look like a blogging plateform, it was originally meant to be an all-platform + sharebale version of notapad++. Meaning that your articles/blogs are stored on cloud and you can (and others can) search them if they want to. Also, I intended it to be available offline, just like nodtepad++. So that you can create and edit your content offline as well.

CodeConvict is just a milestone in the way to the original idea. I use Codeconvict to host my own blogs/notes.

Technologies: Angular 4, Bootstrap 4, HTML/CSS, NodeJS, Express, MongoDB, Heroku

OneTabPlus

Imagine the time when you leave your office to home and wish that you could somehow save your browsing session and reopen them in your home laptop. OneTabPlus does exactly that. You Just need to click on OneTabPlus Icon while leaving for the day and sync chrome using google sign in. Your session will be open as it was.

Technologies: Chrome extensions, Jquery.
